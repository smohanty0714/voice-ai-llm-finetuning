⚡ The Problem Landscape — Why Voice AI Feels Broken
Setting context: these are the real-world failure modes consumers experience daily. Each maps to a specific fine-tuning opportunity we address in subsequent tabs.
PROBLEM 01 — STT

The Destructive Filter

Traditional STT acts as a lossy compression — strips sighs, rising pitch, hesitation, and emotional tone. A panicked "HELP me!" and a calm "help me" become the same text string to the LLM.

PROBLEM 02 — STT

Noise Fragility

Models trained on clean studio audio collapse in real environments — cafés, cars, wind. WER spikes 3-5× in noisy conditions. Consumer voice happens in messy acoustic spaces, not quiet labs.

PROBLEM 03 — STT

Accent & Speaker Diversity Gap

Most ASR training data comes from narrow demographics. Code-switching, regional accents, non-native speakers, and elderly voices suffer disproportionately high WER — creating exclusion in consumer products.

PROBLEM 04 — LLM

The "Deaf LLM" Problem

LLMs process WHAT was said but are completely blind to HOW. Can't detect sarcasm in tone, urgency in pace, or sadness in pitch. The response to a frustrated customer sounds identical to a happy one.

PROBLEM 05 — LLM

SFT Catastrophic Forgetting

When fine-tuning LLMs for speech modality, models "forget" their text capabilities. Training on audio tasks degrades core reasoning. Joint speech-text SFT with LoRA is required to maintain both.

PROBLEM 06 — LLM

Latency vs. Intelligence

Larger models (70B+) deliver better reasoning but add 100-300ms TTFT. Human turn-taking is 500-700ms. Exceeding 1,200ms feels broken. 40%+ users abandon at >1.5s. Smart-and-slow or fast-and-shallow?

PROBLEM 07 — TTS

The "News Anchor" Problem

Most TTS produces flat, uniformly professional delivery — death notifications sound like weather updates. SFT alone cannot teach emotional acting. Models need preference optimization (DPO) to learn expressiveness.

PROBLEM 08 — TTS

Autoregressive Latency Tax

Traditional TTS generates audio tokens one-by-one (left-to-right). Sequential generation adds unacceptable latency and causes repetition/hallucination artifacts at conversational pace.

PROBLEM 09 — PIPELINE

Cascaded Error Propagation

Each handoff in STT→LLM→TTS compounds errors. STT misrecognition feeds wrong text to LLM; LLM responds without acoustic context; TTS synthesizes without knowing user's emotional state. Errors multiply.

PROBLEM 10 — DATA

Audio SFT Data Scarcity

High-quality annotated spoken prompts with emotional context, noise variability, and intent labels are extremely scarce. Most datasets are clean studio ASR data — not representative of real consumer interactions.

🎯 VAD — Voice Activity Detection
"Is someone speaking right now?" — a tiny classifier on every 30ms frame that controls everything downstream. The single biggest latency lever in the entire pipeline.

Architecture

Input: Raw audio, 30ms chunks (512 samples @ 16kHz)

Model: Lightweight RNN/CNN (~1MB). CPU-only, <1ms/frame.

Output: Probability 0→1 (speech confidence). Threshold ~0.5

Endpointing: 200ms silence → "turn complete" → trigger LLM

Why It's the Biggest Lever

200ms = 37% of E2E latency. Reducing to 150ms saves 50ms but causes false turn cuts.

Modern approach: Replace acoustic VAD with semantic endpointing — LLM decides turn completion. Handles mid-thought pauses.

Adaptive: Different thresholds per user + environment. Noisy room ≠ quiet office.
